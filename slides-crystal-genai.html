why generative?

Crystal-structure prediction vs. learning \(p(\mathbf{x})\)

Classical CSP: search

  • Explore the potential-energy surface per composition: genetic algorithms (USPEX), particle swarm (CALYPSO), random search, minima hopping
  • Every candidate needs an explicit energy evaluation — expensive at DFT accuracy
  • Chemical composition & atoms per cell usually fixed a priori
  • Search space grows exponentially with system size

Generative AI: sample

  • Learn structural motifs & bonding rules implicitly from large crystal databases
  • Propose novel, plausible structures directly — no a priori constraints on chemistry or stoichiometry
  • Bypass the iterative search + initial energy-evaluation loop
  • Condition on targets: \(p(\mathbf{x}\mid c)\) → proactive in silico design, not trial and error

Source: Section I, arXiv:2509.02723.

the core idea · Fig. 1

Materials live where energy is low: \(p(\mathbf{x}) \propto e^{-E(\mathbf{x})/k_B T}\)

  • Training data = known structures ≈ samples near energy minima
  • Deep wells → stable phases; shallow wells → metastable phases
  • Low \(T\): probability collapses onto the ground state. Higher \(T\): metastable modes appear
  • The generative model learns this multimodal \(p(\mathbf{x})\) implicitly — no explicit energies needed at sampling time

Toy 1-D energy landscape; concept from Fig. 1 and Sec. II.A, arXiv:2509.02723.

representing crystals

A crystal = lattice \(\mathbf{L}\) + basis: the \((\mathbf{A},\mathbf{X},\mathbf{L})\) picture

  • Species \(\mathbf{A}=\{s_i\}\), coordinates \(\mathbf{X}\), lattice \(\mathbf{L}=(a,b,c,\alpha,\beta,\gamma)\)
  • Fractional coordinates live in the unit cell — change the lattice and every periodic image follows
  • Invertible: from \((\mathbf{A},\mathbf{X},\mathbf{L})\) the full crystal is reconstructed exactly
  • Wanted invariances: translation, rotation, permutation — strict or learned via augmentation

Source: Sec. II.D.1 (point cloud / AXL representation), arXiv:2509.02723.

Generative modeling needs invertible representations — exact reconstruction of the crystal, unlike supervised descriptors. Reciprocal space: \(F(hkl)=\sum_j f_j\, e^{-2\pi i (hx_j + ky_j + lz_j)}\).

Source: Sec. II.D, arXiv:2509.02723 (230 space groups, 1 771 Wyckoff positions).

architecture zoo · Fig. 2

Six ways to learn \(p(\mathbf{x})\)

VAE

\(\max\; \mathbb{E}[\log p_\theta(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z})] - D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi \Vert p)\)

Encode to a smooth latent space, decode to reconstruct; sample the prior for new structures.

GAN

\(\min_G \max_D\; \mathbb{E}[\log D] + \mathbb{E}[\log(1{-}D(G))]\)

Generator vs. discriminator game; no explicit \(p(\mathbf{x})\), prone to mode collapse.

Transformer

\(p(\mathbf{x}) = \prod_i p(x_i \mid \mathbf{x}_{<i})\)

Autoregressive over tokens — atoms, bonds, symmetry descriptors as sequences.

Normalizing flow

\(\log p_{\mathbf x}(\mathbf x) = \log p_{\mathbf z}(f^{-1}(\mathbf x)) + \log\bigl|\det \tfrac{\partial f^{-1}}{\partial \mathbf x}\bigr|\)

Invertible maps, exact likelihood; flow matching gives a simple training objective.

Diffusion

\(\mathcal{L} = \mathbb{E}\,\bigl[\Vert \boldsymbol\epsilon - \boldsymbol\epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t)\Vert^2\bigr]\)

Noise data to Gaussian, learn to denoise step by step; DDIM / score-based SDE variants.

Fine-tuned LLM

pretrained → fine-tuned on CIF

Treat crystallographic files as language; learn the "grammar" of crystal structures.

Source: Fig. 2 and Sec. II.C, arXiv:2509.02723.

diffusion, hands on

Forward noising \(q(\mathbf{x}_t|\mathbf{x}_{t-1})\) · reverse denoising \(p_\theta(\mathbf{x}_{t-1}|\mathbf{x}_t)\)

  • A 2-species toy crystal: atoms diffuse to an isotropic Gaussian, then are pulled back onto the lattice
  • CDVAE (2022): the foundational model — Langevin denoising of coordinates + types, SE(3)-equivariant GNN score
  • DiffCSP: joint diffusion of lattice + fractional coordinates, no VAE
  • MatterGen: diffuses fractional coordinates with a wrapped normal — periodicity respected by construction

Source: Sec. II.C.5 (DDPM), Sec. IV.B (CDVAE, DiffCSP, MatterGen), arXiv:2509.02723.

the surprise contender

LLMs write crystals: CIF as a language

  • XYZTransformer (2023): GPT-like model on XYZ/CIF/PDB text — valid structures without domain-specific representations
  • CrystalTextLLM: Llama-2 70B fine-tuned on textual point clouds, digit-by-digit number tokens, flexible text conditioning + infilling
  • CrystaLLM: LLaMA-style model trained from scratch on CIFs — ≈53% symmetry-valid, ≈17% match known prototypes
  • GenMS: LLM proposes formulas from natural language, diffusion builds the 3-D structure — beats direct CIF prompting
  • MatExpert: conversational agent that retrieves a similar material, proposes edits, and explains its reasoning
  • MatLLMSearch: LLM as crossover/mutation operator inside evolutionary search — no fine-tuning at all

the flavor of a CIF "sentence" an LLM learns to complete:

Prompt-conditioned generation: describe composition, symmetry, even target properties in plain text.

steering generation

From \(p(\mathbf{x})\) to \(p(\mathbf{x} \mid c)\): inverse design

What \(c\) can be

  • Composition — a chemical formula or element set
  • Symmetry — space group / crystal system (DiffCSP++, CrystalFormer sample Wyckoff descriptors per space group)
  • Scalar properties — band gap, formation energy, density, pressure (Cond-CDVAE, CrystalFlow)
  • Free text — e.g. "LiMnO₄ with orthorhombic structure" (TGDMat, Chemeleon)

How it's injected

  • Condition vector into latent space + Langevin dynamics (Cond-CDVAE)
  • Lightweight adapter modules in the score network — property control without retraining the full model (MatterGen)
  • Guided masking of diffusion denoising steps (SCIGEN)
  • Plain prompting for LLM-based models

Source: Sec. II.B, IV.B–D, VI.B, arXiv:2509.02723.

benchmarks · Sec. V

How models are judged — and why comparisons fail

The metric stack

  • Validity — charge balance, sensible distances, symmetry (>90% typical, says little about stability)
  • Stability — energy above convex hull, e.g. <100 meV/atom; DFT or, increasingly, uMLIP relaxation
  • Uniqueness (in-batch) · Novelty (vs. training set) · rediscovery rate
  • S.U.N. — stable ∧ unique ∧ novel, the combined headline metric
  • Efficiency — TGDMat: 500 epochs vs. >3000 for CDVAE / DiffCSP

The review's critique

  • No universal E-hull threshold or shared reference hull — cross-paper numbers don't compare
  • In-house random splits instead of standardized test sets
  • Uniqueness/novelty depend on batch size & matching algorithm
  • Many papers use formation energy without a proper hull at all
  • Consequence: the review deliberately refuses to rank models

Source: Sec. V, arXiv:2509.02723.

honest accounting · Sec. VII

Limitations & where the field is going

Today's blind spots

  • Fully ordered crystals at \(T = 0\) K, full site occupancy — no alloys, vacancies, point defects
  • Data biased toward small unit cells, certain chemistries, GGA energetics
  • Design-space regions unexplored: diffusion in reciprocal space, flows on Wyckoff positions
  • Fragmented benchmarks; synthesizability rarely assessed

Future directions

  • Symmetry, charge neutrality & minimal distances by construction (crystallographic manifolds)
  • Couple to energy surrogates: \(p(\mathbf{x}) \propto e^{-\beta E_\phi(\mathbf{x})}\) via uMLIPs, EBMs, RL, GFlowNets
  • Multi-objective & natural-language conditioning; Pareto-front sampling
  • Community benchmarks: frozen convex hulls, fixed test sets, reported compute cost
  • Generative models as seed proposers in autonomous discovery loops — not one-shot oracles

Source: Sec. VII, arXiv:2509.02723.

takeaways

What to remember

  • Paradigm shift: stop searching \(E(\mathbf{x})\) composition by composition — learn \(p(\mathbf{x}) \propto e^{-E/k_BT}\) from databases and sample it
  • Representation is the real design choice: point clouds, voxels, graphs, reciprocal space, Wyckoff positions — invertibility and symmetry decide what a model can do
  • Diffusion on point clouds + graphs dominates (CDVAE → DiffCSP → MatterGen); Wyckoff models buy symmetry; LLMs are surprisingly capable crystal writers
  • Conditioning turns generation into inverse design — composition, space group, properties, even free text
  • Benchmarking is the weak point: 61 models, no standardized S.U.N. protocol — the review refuses to crown a winner
