01 · Motivation

Materials science is drowning in its own literature

  • Materials innovation touches 10 of 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals — but insight is buried in unstructured text and semi-structured tables.
  • General LLMs misread physical laws, specialized terminology, crystal structures — critical analyses find them unreliable for real materials discovery.
  • Proprietary frontier models are costly to run over millions of articles — deployable open models are needed.
  • Prior finetunes solved single tasks; LLaMat aims at one foundation: NLP + extraction + crystal generation.

04 · Benchmarks — extraction from text

Structured extraction: text → JSON schemas

  • Nine tasks over doping, MOFs, general materials — answers must parse as JSON against a fixed schema.
  • LLaMat-chat models are strongest on host–dopant and formula–application relations; MOFs stay hard for everyone (best exact match just 0.118, tied by LLaMat-2-chat and finetuned LLaMA baselines).
Source: Tables E.1–E.2 (SIE Doping / MOFs), Fig. 3a–b, §4.1.4.
04 · Benchmarks — extraction from tables

DiSCoMaT: 737 real composition tables → JSON

  • LLaMat wins where domain notation rules (regex-style tables: 0.878 vs ≤0.47 for all commercial models); commercial LLMs win material-ID and composition localization.
  • On whole-table exact match, LLaMat-2-chat gets 75%; GPT-4o 7%, Claude-3.5-Sonnet 9%, Claude-3-Haiku 0% — percentages are of JSON-parseable outputs and denominators differ per model (LLaMat-3-chat parses only 602 of 737; raw counts in bar tooltips).
Source: Table E.2 (DiSCoMaT), Fig. 3c–d; dataset §4.1.4 [38].

06 · The headline finding — why?

Hypothesis: overtrained minima resist re-shaping

  • LLaMA-3’s far larger pretraining (paper: “~3 orders of magnitude more data”) may leave it saturated — great baseline, poor clay.
  • Hypothesis: the loss landscape near overtrained minima has a “notably different character” [49] — harder to re-shape via CPT.
  • Symptoms everywhere, even the CPT loss curve: LLaMat-2 descends smoothly, LLaMat-3 stays higher and spikier (Fig. C.1). Named “adaptation rigidity” — first report; cf. [35, 36].
Source: §2.2, Discussion. The widget is a schematic illustration of the hypothesis — not data from the paper.
Implications

What this means for scientific LLMs

  • Pick bases for adaptability, not leaderboard rank. A saturated model can be the worse starting point for CPT.
  • 7B open + ~30B domain tokens + staged IFT beats GPT-4-class on domain tasks — at deployable cost (8×A100 × 17 days, or ~3 days on 2 Cerebras CS-2s).
  • Guard the mix: a ~3% replay slice preserves English; dropping MathQA breaks even elementary arithmetic (Table D.3).
  • Domain knowledge and instruction-following are separable: extra domain IFT (Honeybee) added nothing after Orca + MathQA.
  • LLMs can be generative crystallographers: text-trained models learn charge neutrality and lattice motifs implicitly.
Source: Discussion; App. D ablations (Tables D.2–D.3).
Limitations

Read the fine print

  • Only 7–8B models — 70B “could yield superior performance” but was out of compute scope.
  • Lineages aren’t perfectly matched: 31.5B CPT tokens for LLaMat-2 vs 10.9B for LLaMat-3, different tokenizers and hardware — rigidity is confounded, not isolated.
  • “Adaptation rigidity” is a hypothesis: no loss-landscape measurement or causal test yet.
  • SIE scoring counts only JSON-parseable outputs; no human evaluation was performed.
  • LLaMat-3-CIF needed ~33k generations for 10k usable structures (2.5× LLaMat-2-CIF), and crystal finetuning is hyperparameter-sensitive.
  • Commercial LLMs still lead on table composition/material-ID localization tasks.
Source: §2.1–2.3, §4.1.5, App. C–D.
Takeaways

Three things to remember

  • Domain CPT works. Two mid-size open models become materials copilots that outscore GPT-4o, Claude-3.5-Sonnet and Gemini-1.5-Pro on materials NLP (93 vs ≤68 micro-F1) and table extraction (75% vs ≤20% exact match).
  • The mix is the product. 94% papers + small, deliberate CIF / replay / math slices — each ablates badly if removed.
  • More pretraining ≠ more adaptable. LLaMat-2 (from the weaker LLaMA-2) beats LLaMat-3 after identical adaptation — test your base before you commit.
